TERMS OF SERVICE AND CONSENT TO PARTICIPATE IN

NON-BIOMEDICAL RESEARCH

 

Terra Incognita: A Personal Media Geography Browser

 

 

 

You are asked to participate in a research study conducted by Catherine D'Ignazio and Ethan Zuckerman, from the Center for Civic Media at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (M.I.T.) The data collected during this research is part of Catherine D'Ignazio's Masters thesis research.  You were selected as a possible participant in this study because of your background in journalism and commitment to a balanced and fair public sphere of discourse. We seek to develop this application further with your feedback. You should read the information below, and ask questions about anything you do not understand, before deciding whether or not to participate.

 

 

    PARTICIPATION AND WITHDRAWAL

 

Your participation in this study is completely voluntary and you are free to choose whether to be in it or not. If you choose to be in this study, you may subsequently withdraw from it at any time without penalty or consequences of any kind.  The investigator may withdraw you from this research if circumstances arise which warrant doing so. 

 

    PURPOSE OF THE STUDY

 

The purpose of the Terra Incognita browser extension is to determine whether having maps and visualizations showing the user places that they have read about and places that they have not read about would serve as an incentive to explore more geographically diverse information online.

 

 

    PROCEDURES

 

If you volunteer to participate in this study, we would ask you to do the following things:

 

1.    Use the Chrome browser as your primary browser for at least thirty days after installing the browser extension.

 

2.    Read and browse the Internet as you normally would.

 

3.    If they are of interest, examine the maps and visualizations of your browsing data that the application generates for you.

4.    If they are of interest, follow the information recommendations that the application generates for you.

 

 

    POTENTIAL RISKS AND DISCOMFORTS

 

This application is collecting and storing parts your browsing history which is sensitive personal data. We collect and store only your news browsing history. We define news as content from this list of sites. We do NOT review, store or operate on your email, facebook, twitter, amazon.com, google docs, google search history (or anything from google.com) or other personal browsing history. We collect your news browsing history from 45 days prior to using Terra Incognita to 30 days after using Terra Incognita. If your news browser history were released publicly you risk invasion of privacy, embarrassment, and potential exposure of sensitive or confidential data. We do not take these risks lightly and are putting into place a number of safeguards so that your data is protected and so that you have control over your data.

 

The measures we are taking to secure your data on our server include encrypting communications, requiring a robust authentication protocol, storing with industry standard data protection technologies, installing firewalls and other protocol.

 

 You have the ability to download your data and metadata at any time by emailing the principal investigators. Additionally, you may choose to delete your data from our server or withdraw from the study at any time without any negative consequences.

 

 

    POTENTIAL BENEFITS

 

Your potential benefits from the study primarily include insight into your own behavior: What countries do you read about most often? What topics do you read about in relation to those countries? Do these match what is covered by mainstream media? Are you missing out on critical news coverage by not reading geographically diverse information? Based on your reading behavior there is a recommendation component to the system, so you will be are given recommendations to read about and explore places that you have not previously encountered. The benefit of this is potentially a more well-rounded and well-informed member of society.

 

This study is being undertaken because of the increasing risks of polarization in a world where we get most of our information from search engines, friends, or recommendation engines. While these are great technologies, they also serve to create echo chambers where people encounter only information from others like themselves. Our study wishes to determine whether providing people with personal analytics and visualizations about the geographic diversity of their information consumption might provide enough incentive to take risks and encounter new and more geographically diverse information. While the scope of the study is small, the potential impact of this kind of work is great and includes a better informed, less polarized, and more engaged populace.

 

    PAYMENT FOR PARTICIPATION

 

There is no payment for participation.

 

 

    CONFIDENTIALITY

 

Any information that is obtained in connection with this study and that can be identified with you will remain confidential and will be disclosed only with your permission or as required by law.

 

We are taking robust measures to secure your data including encrypting communications, requiring a robust authentication protocol, storing with industry standard data protection technologies, installing firewalls and other protocol.

 

When the study is over we will destroy the browsing history data but retain the metadata created during processing. This includes countries visited and keywords associated with different countries.

 

Publication forums for this research may include research journals, news articles about the research and the MIT Center for Civic Media blog. Neither individual nor aggregate browsing histories will be published in any form. We plan to publish maps and visualizations of the metadata generated by text processing. This includes countries read about and the keywords associated with different countries, but not browsing histories. Your identifying information would never be associated with the metadata that we publish.
